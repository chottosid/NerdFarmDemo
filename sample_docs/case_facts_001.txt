CASE FACT SUMMARY

Case: Smith v. Johnson Properties LLC
Case Number: 2024-CV-0456
Court: Cook County Circuit Court
Date Prepared: March 20, 2024

PARTIES

Plaintiff: Martha Smith
- Owner of 456 Oak Avenue, Chicago, IL
- Purchased property in August 2020
- Retired school teacher, 68 years old

Defendant: Johnson Properties LLC
- Property development company
- Constructed adjacent commercial building in 2022
- Principal: Robert Johnson

BACKGROUND

In August 2020, Plaintiff Martha Smith purchased a single-family home at 456 Oak Avenue. The property had been in her family for three generations and she intended to live there during her retirement.

In March 2022, Defendant Johnson Properties LLC began construction of a commercial office building on the adjacent lot at 450 Oak Avenue. The construction was completed in December 2022.

KEY EVENTS TIMELINE

- August 15, 2020: Smith purchases property
- March 1, 2022: Johnson Properties begins construction
- June 2022: Smith reports foundation cracks in her home
- September 2022: Smith hires structural engineer
- October 2022: Engineer report confirms foundation damage
- December 2022: Construction completed
- January 2023: Smith files formal complaint with city
- March 2023: City inspection confirms code violations
- June 2023: Smith demands compensation; Johnson refuses
- February 2024: Smith files lawsuit

CLAIMS

1. Negligence - Defendant failed to take reasonable precautions during construction
2. Nuisance - Construction caused ongoing disruption and damage
3. Trespass - Physical damage to plaintiff's property
4. Property Damage - Foundation repairs estimated at $85,000

EVIDENCE

- Structural engineer report (October 2022)
- City inspection report (March 2023)
- Photographs of damage
- Construction permits and plans
- Expert testimony scheduled

DAMAGES SOUGHT

- Foundation repair costs: $85,000
- Temporary housing during repairs: $15,000
- Diminished property value: $45,000
- Emotional distress: $25,000
- Total: $170,000

STATUS

Case is currently in discovery phase. Mediation scheduled for April 15, 2024.
