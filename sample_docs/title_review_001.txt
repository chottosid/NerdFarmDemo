Prepared by: Pearson Specter Litt - Title Division

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This title review was conducted for the proposed acquisition of the subject property. Our examination revealed a clear title with minor exceptions as noted below.

CHAIN OF TITLE

The subject property was originally patented to the United States Government in 1837. The chain of title is as follows:

1. Warranty Deed dated January 15, 1952, from Robert and Margaret Johnson to William and Helen Thompson, recorded in Book 234, Page 56.

2. Warranty Deed dated March 3, 1978, from the Estate of William Thompson to Patricia Davis, recorded in Book 456, Page 78.

3. Warranty Deed dated June 22, 1995, from Patricia Davis to Michael and Sarah Chen, recorded in Book 678, Page 90.

4. Warranty Deed dated September 10, 2019, from Michael and Sarah Chen to current owners, David and Jennifer Martinez, recorded in Book 890, Page 112.

ENCUMBRANCES AND EXCEPTIONS

1. Easement to Springfield Utility Company for underground utilities, dated April 5, 1960, recorded in Book 300, Page 45.

2. Restrictive covenant limiting construction to single-family residential use only, dated January 15, 1952, recorded in Book 234, Page 57.

3. Property tax lien for 2023 taxes in the amount of $4,567.89, payable by the current owner.

TITLE INSURANCE RECOMMENDATION

Based on our review, we recommend issuing a standard owner's title insurance policy in the amount of the purchase price, subject to the standard exceptions and the specific encumbrances noted above.

CONCLUSION

Title to the subject property is considered marketable, subject to the encumbrances and exceptions noted herein. No serious title defects were identified during our examination.
