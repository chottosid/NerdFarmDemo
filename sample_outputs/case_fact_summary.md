# Case Fact Summary: Smith v. Johnson Properties LLC

## Parties Involved

- **Plaintiff:** Martha Smith, a 68-year-old retired schoolteacher residing at 456 Oak Avenue, Chicago, IL
- **Defendant:** Johnson Properties LLC, a property management company represented by Managing Director Robert Johnson [Source: case_facts_001.txt, Page 1]

## Property Details

The subject property is a residential duplex located at 456 Oak Avenue, Chicago, IL 60614, purchased by the plaintiff in August 2020 for $425,000. [Source: case_facts_001.txt, Page 1]

## Key Facts

### Purchase and Initial Condition
- Ms. Smith purchased the property in "as-is" condition on August 15, 2020
- The purchase was facilitated through Johnson Properties LLC, who acted as both seller and property manager
- Prior to purchase, Ms. Smith was shown only the renovated upper unit [Source: case_facts_001.txt, Page 1]

### Discovery of Defects
Within 60 days of purchase, Ms. Smith discovered the following issues:
- Significant water damage in the lower unit basement
- Mold growth affecting approximately 40% of the lower unit
- Non-functional HVAC system in the lower unit
- Previous flooding evidence not disclosed during sale [Source: case_facts_001.txt, Page 1]

### Defendant's Knowledge
Evidence suggests Johnson Properties LLC had prior knowledge of defects:
- Internal emails dated March 2020 discussed "basement water issues"
- A maintenance worker's testimony confirms repeated flooding in spring 2020
- Plumbing inspection reports from April 2020 were withheld from the buyer [Source: case_facts_001.txt, Page 1]

## Damages Claimed

- Cost of mold remediation: $18,500
- HVAC replacement: $8,200
- Structural repairs: $22,000
- Temporary housing costs: $4,800
- **Total damages claimed: $53,500** [Source: case_facts_001.txt, Page 1]

## Legal Claims

1. **Fraudulent Concealment** - Defendant knowingly hid material defects
2. **Breach of Implied Warranty of Habitability** - Property was uninhabitable
3. **Negligent Misrepresentation** - False statements about property condition
4. **Violation of Illinois Residential Real Property Disclosure Act** - Failure to disclose known defects [Source: case_facts_001.txt, Page 1]

## Procedural History

- Complaint filed: October 12, 2020
- Case number: 2020-CV-00345
- Assigned to: Circuit Court of Cook County, Judge Margaret Chen
- Status: Discovery phase ongoing [Source: case_facts_001.txt, Page 1]
